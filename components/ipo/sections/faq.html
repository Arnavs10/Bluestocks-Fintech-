Frequently Asked Questions?

Find answers to common questions that come in your mind related to IPO.

What is an IPO? +

IPO or Initial Public Offering is the first time a company issues its shares to the public. As an investor, you will now be able to subscribe for such shares, which was earlier open to only a specific set of several and institutional investors via opening a Demat account.

How to invest in an IPO? +

To invest in an IPO, you need to have a Demat account. Once you have a Demat account, you can apply for IPOs through your broker's website or app. You will need to fill in details like the number of shares you want to apply for and the price you're willing to pay (if it's a price band).

What is the benefit of an IPO? +

IPOs provide an opportunity to invest in growing companies at an early stage. If the company performs well, the value of your shares can increase significantly over time. IPOs also offer the potential for listing gains, where shares are immediately worth more on the stock market than what you paid during the IPO.

What are the disadvantages of an IPO? +

IPO investments can be risky as the company has no trading history in the public market. The stock price might be volatile in the initial days. Also, there's limited information available about the company compared to established listed companies. Sometimes companies may be overvalued during IPO, leading to potential losses for investors.

What is the difference between book building issue and fixed price issue? +

In a book building issue, investors can bid for shares within a price band set by the company. The final price is determined based on these bids. In a fixed price issue, the company sets a specific price at which investors can buy shares, with no flexibility in pricing.

Is it mandatory to have a PAN number to apply in an IPO? +

Yes, it is mandatory to have a PAN (Permanent Account Number) to apply for an IPO in India. The PAN is required for KYC (Know Your Customer) verification and to track your investments for tax purposes.

Where do I get an IPO application form? +

IPO application forms are available online through your stockbroker's website or mobile app. Most brokers now offer a digital application process where you can apply for IPOs without any physical forms. You can also apply through the ASBA (Application Supported by Blocked Amount) facility of your bank.

How one can apply in IPO's online? +

To apply for an IPO online, log in to your broker's website or app, navigate to the IPO section, select the IPO you want to apply for, enter the number of lots you wish to apply for, confirm your UPI ID, and submit your application. Your funds will be blocked via UPI and will be debited only if shares are allotted to you.

How one can apply in IPO's offline? +

To apply for an IPO offline, you can visit your bank or broker's physical branch and collect an IPO application form (ASBA form). Fill out the form with your personal details, Demat account information, the number of shares you wish to apply for, and your bank account details. Submit the completed form at the designated bank branch before the IPO closing date.

Can a minor apply in an IPO? +

No, minors cannot directly apply for an IPO as they cannot have a trading and Demat account in their own name. However, a guardian can open a Demat account on behalf of a minor and apply for IPOs. Once the minor becomes an adult (18 years), the account can be converted to a regular Demat account.

What is the procedure to withdraw from an IPO? +

To withdraw from an IPO application, you need to approach the same bank branch where you submitted your ASBA form before the IPO closing date. For online applications, you can usually withdraw through your broker's platform or app. The withdrawal facility is available until the IPO closure date, after which applications cannot be withdrawn.

How is IPO return calculated? +

IPO returns are calculated by comparing the IPO allotment price with the current market price. The formula is: ((Current Market Price - IPO Price) / IPO Price) Ã— 100. This gives you the percentage return on your investment. For listing day returns, replace the current market price with the listing day closing price.

Can one apply for an IPO from a sweep in/out saving bank account? +

Yes, you can apply for an IPO using a sweep-in/sweep-out savings bank account. The ASBA mechanism only blocks the required amount in your account until allotment, so as long as the funds are available when needed, the sweep feature won't affect your application. Just ensure sufficient balance is maintained in your account until the IPO allotment process is complete.

What is the minimum and maximum investment one could do in the HNI category? +

The minimum investment for the HNI (High Net-worth Individual) category in an IPO is above Rs. 2 lakhs. There is no upper limit for the maximum investment in the HNI category. HNIs can apply for as many lots as they wish, provided they have sufficient funds available in their linked bank account for blocking.

How to apply more than one application in an IPO? +

SEBI regulations allow retail investors to submit up to 3 applications per IPO using different bank accounts and different Demat accounts. Each application must be in the name of different account holders. Make sure not to exceed the investment limit of Rs. 2 lakhs for the retail category across all applications, as this could lead to disqualification.
