Frequently Asked Questions?

Find answers to common questions that come in your mind related to IPO.

How to Subscribe to an IPO?

-
  • Step 1: Login to your respective service provider.
  • Step 2: Click on the IPO button.
  • Step 3: Select the IPO you want to bid and enter the relevant details.
  • Step4: Your subscription will be completed once you make the payment or give permission.

Should I buy an IPO first day?

+

It depends on your investment strategy and research on the company. There's no definitive rule that the first day is best - sometimes waiting helps you observe market trends, but in high-demand IPOs, early subscription might increase allocation chances.

How do you know if an IPO is good?

+

Evaluate an IPO's quality by analyzing the company's financial health, growth prospects, management team, business model, industry position, and intended use of IPO funds. Also review the prospectus thoroughly and consider the valuation compared to industry peers.

How to check IPO start date?

+

You can check IPO start dates on your broker's website/app, financial news portals like MoneyControl or Economic Times, stock exchange websites (NSE/BSE), or SEBI's website. Companies also announce dates in their Red Herring Prospectus (RHP) and through press releases.

What is issue size?

+

Issue size refers to the total amount of money a company aims to raise through its IPO. It's calculated by multiplying the number of shares offered by the issue price. For example, if a company offers 1 crore shares at ₹100 each, the issue size would be ₹100 crores.

How many shares in a lot?

+

The number of shares in a lot varies for each IPO and is determined by the company. Typically, lot sizes are designed to keep the application amount between ₹10,000-₹15,000 at the upper price band. Common lot sizes range from 15 to 195 shares depending on the share price.

How is the lot size calculated?

+

Lot size is calculated by dividing the desired minimum investment amount (typically ₹10,000-₹15,000) by the upper price band of the IPO. Companies then round this number to make it easily divisible and manageable for investors and the application process.

Who decides the IPO price band?

+

The IPO price band is decided by the company issuing shares in consultation with their book running lead managers (BRLMs) or investment bankers. They consider factors like company valuation, market conditions, investor appetite, comparable company valuations, and future growth prospects.

What is IPO GMP?

+

GMP (Grey Market Premium) is the premium amount at which IPO shares are traded in the unofficial/grey market before they are listed on the stock exchange. It indicates the expected listing price and investor sentiment. For example, if an IPO is priced at ₹100 and has a GMP of ₹30, it suggests the shares might list around ₹130.

How many lots should I apply for IPO?

+

The number of lots you should apply for depends on your investment capacity, risk tolerance, and conviction in the company. Retail investors can apply for a maximum of 13-14 lots in most IPOs. For beginners, starting with 1-2 lots is advisable, while experienced investors might apply for more based on their analysis of the company.
